FAQ

Q 1: After I have completed the training program, where else can I get training?

FCI is working to develop supplemental material to this training program. Check back here for updates in the future and in the meantime, information is available in the FCI Cybrary and on FCI’s blog. Additionally, the education program at FCI’s Annual Conference features sessions pertaining to ERM that will focus on specific skills and details of the process.

Q3: How does FCI monitor ISO/ANSI/ASSE 31000 revisions?

All published ISO standards get reviewed approximately every five years to ensure that they remain “state of the art” by international consensus. FCI is a member on the United States technical advisory committee that is participating in this review and will therefore be monitoring any substantive changes. FCI reviews the program’s curriculum every two years. FCI’s review process will ensure that the training program’s curriculum incorporates any changes made by the ISO revision team.

Q4: How does this course relate to the ARM courses I’ve taken?

The Associate in Risk Management (ARM) is a designation that you earn after taking total of three basic courses (ARM 400, 401 and 402) as well as an ethics exam. These exams are self-directed and uniform. The exams are based on content in textbooks and the knowledge addressed in the texts is meant to give students in depth knowledge about general risk management practices and principles, risk assessment and risk financing. FCI's ERM training, is in-person and designed to provide you with a foundational knowledge of the ISO 31000 Standard. The small workshops will teach you how risk management brings value to your organization and how to easily implement the concepts of ISO 31000 throughout your organization so that you can advance your organization's mission. Workshop attendees receive directives and suggestions from trainers that have years of experience in risk management and have implemented successful programs in their organizations. Attendees will also develop their own plans to either start or improve their own program.
